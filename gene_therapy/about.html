What is Gene Therapy?

Gene therapy involves introducing genetic material into a patient's cells to correct or replace defective genes, provide therapeutic proteins, or modulate gene expression. This can be accomplished using various techniques, including viral vectors, non-viral vectors, and genome editing tools like CRISPR-Cas9.
Gene therapy is a revolutionary approach to treating and potentially curing diseases by targeting the underlying genetic causes. Unlike traditional medical treatments that primarily focus on managing symptoms or replacing deficient proteins, gene therapy aims to address the root genetic abnormalities that lead to disease development.
